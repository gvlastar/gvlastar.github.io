Initiatives
  • Mental Health First Aid:This 8-hour course teaches how to offer initial help in a mental health crisis and connect persons to appropriate resources.
  • Collegiate Recovery Community:The CMU Collegiate Recovery Program (CRC) strives to connect, support, and engage students (and allies) in or seeking recovery from alcohol and other drugs.
  • Green Dot:Green Dot training teaches how to be an active bystander around dating/domestic violence, sexual assault, and stalking in our community.
Services Provided
General Illness or Injury
Nurse and doctor/advanced practice appointments,follow up care,same-day appointments
Comprehensive Care Management
Our Comprehensive Care Manager (CCM) helps students develop plans to identify and coordinate services to meet their health and academic support needs. The CCM works with Academic Development, Counseling and Psychological Services, Disability Services, Housing and Dining Services and other campus offices. This free service is available to all students.
Insurance
Medical, Dental & Vision insurance enrollment,claims assistance,insurance mandate/waiver assistance
LGBTQ Health
LGBTQ Sensitive General Medical Care, Pre-Exposure Prophylaxis (PrEP), Hormone Therapy (we use an informed consent model)
Psychiatric Services
We have a psychiatrist who works in collaboration with CaPS clinicians. All referrals for psychiatric treatment are made through Counseling and Psychological Services (CaPS). Please contact CaPS at 412-268-2922 for referral information.
Vaccinations and Travel Health
Vaccines: Hepatitis A, Hepatitis B, HPV (Gardasil), MMR, Menningitis, Tetanus, Tuberculosis Skin Testing, Anti-Malarial medication, for travel health information for specific destinations, visit the CDC Traveler's website, The Allegheny County Health Department offers additional vaccines and travel consultations
